Section 01
Structural Overview: The Core Difference

The existing schema was built to categorise recommendations from the first 8–10 reports with a compact 6×3 grid. The new schema adds 2 wholly new themes (Legal & Governance; Capital Account & INR Internationalisation) and gives every existing theme a fourth sub-theme to capture material that was previously either unclassified or awkwardly stuffed into adjacent buckets.

Section 02
Key Structural Decisions Explained

Three principal architectural choices differentiate the new schema from the old.

🔀
Renaming: Remittances & Capital Flight → Remittances, Repatriation & NRI
The old label "Capital Flight" implied a pejorative view of outward flows. The new name is more neutral and descriptive, and explicitly names "NRI" as a constituency — correctly reflecting that ~40% of recommendations in this theme come from NRI/PIO-specific committee reports. The old label also conflated monitoring (AML) with policy (LRS), which the new 4-sub-theme structure separates cleanly.
🆕
Two Entirely New Themes Created
Legal & Governance absorbs FEMA reform, judicial review, BIT/ISDS recommendations that were previously scattered across FDI, FPI, and SR themes. Capital Account & INR Internationalisation collects CAC sequencing, rupee internationalisation, IFSC/GIFT City, and cross-border payments content that had no home in the old schema — particularly important for Tarapore (both), Mistry, Ratho INR 2022, and FSLRC reports.
✂️
P-Notes Carved Out of DMI → Moved to FPI
In the old schema, Participatory Notes / ODIs sat inside "OTC Markets & P-Notes" under Derivatives & Market Infrastructure. This is analytically wrong — P-Notes are a foreign investor access mechanism, not a derivatives instrument. The new schema moves them to the FPI theme as a dedicated 4th sub-theme, while "OTC Markets" in the DMI theme now correctly focuses on NDF markets, forward contracts, and onshore-offshore integration.

FDI Foreign Direct Investment — 3 old sub-themes → 4 new sub-themes
FDI Entry Routes & Sectoral Caps Entry Routes, Negative Lists & Sectoral Caps Expanded
"Negative Lists" explicitly added to the sub-theme title. The old label implied purely quantitative cap management; the new label captures the policy instrument of negative-listing (as opposed to a positive/sectoral-cap approach). Also adds Press Note 3 (land-bordering countries) scope.
Example: Mistry 2007 recommended eliminating protectionist sectoral caps. The old schema captured this but not the specific negative-list vs. automatic route distinction that the Consolidated FDI Policy uses.
FDI Procedural Simplification Approval Processes & Procedural Simplification Expanded The new name leads with "Approval Processes", foregrounding FIPB → RBI delegated authority as the central mechanism. "Procedural Simplification" was accurate but vague; the new name specifies what processes are being simplified. Also covers two-tier approval tiers and AD-level processing explicitly.
Example: Tarapore 1997's recommendation to replace prior RBI approval with AD-level reporting; Chandrasekhar 2013's automatic DDP registration — both map here, now more clearly framed as approval-process reform.
FDI Pricing & Valuation Norms Pricing, Valuation & Transfer Pricing Norms Expanded "Transfer Pricing" added. The old sub-theme focused on the regulatory floor price for FDI transactions (DCF vs. market-price). The new sub-theme adds related-party transaction disclosure and transfer pricing concerns — a key gap flagged for the later reports (H.R. Khan 2019 era onward).
Example: Sahoo DR 2013's recommendation to dispense with the discounted cash flow pricing norm for unlisted securities sits here; the new label correctly links this to the broader transfer-pricing discipline.
FDI No equivalent Downstream Investment & Structural Restrictions NEW New Addition Entirely new sub-theme. Covers FOCC (Foreign Owned and Controlled Company) classification, the 75:25 deferred payment framework for downstream investments, share-swap rules, and DR issuance / two-way fungibility norms. These were previously unclassified or squeezed into "Entry Routes" despite being structurally distinct.
Example: The RBI Master Direction (2025) introducing the 75:25 deferred payment framework — clearly belongs here, not under "Entry Routes" or "Procedural Simplification."
FPI Foreign Portfolio Investment — 3 old sub-themes → 4 new sub-themes
FPI Registration & Classification Investor Classification, Registration & Onboarding Expanded "Onboarding" added. The old label implied a static registration event; the new label captures the full onboarding journey — DDP/SWAGAT-FI single window, digital-signature KYC, Category I/II/III risk tiers. The word "Onboarding" directly maps to the SEBI FPI 2022 report's focus on streamlining documentation and electronic signatures.
Example: SEBI FPI 2022's 5 recommendations on digital signatures, SWIFT-based certification, and CAF portal PAN verification — "registration" alone didn't capture this operational onboarding dimension.
FPI Investment Limits & Monitoring Investment Limits, Sectoral Ceilings & Monitoring Expanded "Sectoral Ceilings" added to explicitly distinguish portfolio-level aggregate limits (24% FPI ceiling) from instrument-specific sub-ceilings (FAR/VRR for debt, sector-wise equity caps). The old label bundled both, making it harder to distinguish FPI equity from FPI debt flow management.
Example: Ratho INR 2022's recommendation that all G-secs be under FAR, and the VRR corporate bond limit be set as a percentage — this is a "Sectoral Ceiling" refinement, not just a monitoring change.
FPI KYC & Compliance Framework KYC, AML & Disclosure Compliance Expanded "AML" and "Disclosure" explicitly added. The old label used "Compliance Framework" as a catch-all; the new label specifies OECD/FATF anti-money-laundering obligations and beneficial-ownership disclosure / look-through requirements as distinct sub-components. These are increasingly the substance of global regulatory reform.
Example: Chandrasekhar 2013's recommendation for a PMLA risk-based KYC master file; the look-through requirements for Category III FPIs. The new label makes AML a first-class concept, not an afterthought inside "compliance."
FPI Partial: was in DMI "OTC Markets & P-Notes" Participatory Notes, ODIs & Offshore Derivative Instruments Carved In Carved In Critical re-classification. P-Notes/ODIs were previously under "OTC Markets & P-Notes" in the DMI theme — an analytically incorrect placement because P-Notes are not a derivatives market instrument; they are a foreign investor access mechanism. The new schema correctly relocates them to the FPI theme. The DMI "OTC Markets" sub-theme now focuses purely on NDF/onshore-offshore rate dynamics.
Example: Tarapore 2006's recommendation to ban fresh P-Note inflows is an FPI access restriction — it has nothing to do with OTC derivatives market structure. Previously, this sat in the wrong theme entirely.
ECB External Commercial Borrowings — 3 old sub-themes → 4 new sub-themes
ECB Borrowing Limits & Maturity Eligibility, Borrowing Limits & Maturity Structure Expanded "Eligibility" added upfront. Sahoo ECB 2015 and the 2026 RBI Master Direction both have significant eligible-borrower provisions (LLPs, IBC entities, IFC-based SPVs) that were being classified under "Borrowing Limits" despite being logically about who can borrow, not how much. The new label creates a proper three-part hierarchy: Eligibility → Limits → Maturity.
Example: The 2026 RBI Master Direction making LLPs eligible ECB borrowers — this is an eligibility change, not a borrowing limit change; the old sub-theme didn't distinguish these.
ECB End-Use Restrictions End-Use Restrictions & Negative List Expanded "Negative List" added. Sahoo ECB 2015's seminal recommendation was that the FDI negative list should be the only negative list for ECB — i.e., end-use restrictions should be unified with FDI policy. The old label "End-Use Restrictions" captured the existence of restrictions but not this crucial policy linkage to the FDI negative list.
Example: Sahoo ECB 2015: "The ECB policy should use the FDI negative list as its only negative list." This recommendation was being coded under "End-Use Restrictions" with no indication that it was a negative-list alignment recommendation.
ECB Currency Risk & Hedging Currency Risk, Hedging Mandates & FCCB Expanded "Mandates" and "FCCB" added. Sahoo ECB 2015's key innovation was a uniform statutory hedge-ratio determined by a MoF-RBI Committee — a "mandate" with legal force, not merely guidance. FCCB conversion-price reset restrictions are a distinct instrument-level concern previously not captured. The old label implied voluntary risk management; the new label captures compulsory regulatory hedging requirements.
Example: Sahoo ECB 2015: "Every ECB borrower must hedge a uniform percentage of currency exposure." This is a hedging mandate, qualitatively different from the Tarapore 1997 recommendation to simply permit forward contracts — but both coded under the same old sub-theme.
ECB No equivalent ECB Policy Architecture & Reporting NEW New Addition Entirely new sub-theme. Captures event-based Forms ECB 1 & 2, AD bank enforcement responsibility, reporting timelines, and cost-of-borrowing liberalisation provisions. These are the governance/plumbing of the ECB framework — they don't naturally belong under eligibility, end-use, or hedging, but they matter enormously for implementation. Previously these were unclassified.
Example: Sahoo ECB 2015: "Authorities must prescribe ECB-related regulations only through formal regulations with a stated rationale." This is an ECB policy governance recommendation with no home in the old schema's 3 sub-themes.
Remittances Remittances, Repatriation & NRI Framework (was: Remittances & Capital Flight) — 3 old sub-themes → 4 new
RCF Individual Remittance Limits Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) & Resident Outward Investment Renamed Renamed to use the actual regulatory instrument name (LRS) that this sub-theme describes. "Individual Remittance Limits" was descriptive but generic; "LRS" is the branded framework used in every subsequent committee report and regulatory update. "Resident Outward Investment" adds the dimension of overseas securities investment via LRS beyond simple remittance.
Example: UK Sinha 2010's recommendation to allow derivatives investment abroad under LRS; Tarapore 2006's phased $25k→$200k LRS limit increase — now correctly labelled as LRS recommendations rather than generic "individual limits."
RCF NRI/PIO Investment Framework NRI/PIO Investment Schemes & Deposit Frameworks Expanded "Deposit Frameworks" added. Tarapore 1997's recommendation to terminate the NRNRRD scheme and rationalise NRE/NRO/SNRR accounts is fundamentally a deposit architecture recommendation, not just an investment-limit recommendation. The old label missed this. Ratho INR 2022's recommendation for a single SNRR account — clearly a deposit framework reform.
Example: Tarapore 1997: "Discontinue the NRNRRD scheme." Ratho INR 2022: "A single SNRR account should cover all permissible transactions." These are deposit-framework recommendations, not captured by the old "NRI/PIO Investment Framework" label alone.
RCF Repatriation Norms Repatriation, EEFC Accounts & Export Earnings Expanded "EEFC Accounts" and "Export Earnings" explicitly added. Tarapore 1997's 100% EEFC retention recommendation is a key reform that was being coded as a generic "repatriation norm" when it is specifically about exporters' foreign currency retention rights. Ratho INR 2022's Masala bond withholding tax waiver and export INR invoicing incentive — both export-earnings recommendations — now have a named home.
Example: Tarapore 1997: "Allow exporters 100% EEFC retention with cheque writing facility." Ratho INR 2022: "Waive withholding tax on Masala bond issuances." Both are export-earnings repatriation recommendations now clearly labelled.
RCF No equivalent Capital Flight Monitoring & AML Compliance NEW New Addition Entirely new sub-theme. The old theme was called "Remittances & Capital Flight" but had no sub-theme actually dedicated to monitoring or AML. This new sub-theme captures AD bank real-time monitoring obligations, Enforcement Directorate reporting, PMLA transaction thresholds, and anti-money-laundering integration — all of which appear across multiple reports but had no classification home.
Example: Chandrasekhar 2013's PMLA/KYC risk-based approach recommendations and the Enforcement Directorate's role in capital flight investigation — previously unclassified in the RCF theme, now given a dedicated home.
Derivatives & Infra Derivatives, Market Infrastructure & Currency Markets — 3 old sub-themes → 4 new sub-themes
DMI Exchange-Traded Derivatives Exchange-Traded Currency & Interest Rate Derivatives Expanded Asset classes explicitly specified: "Currency & Interest Rate." The old label was fine for Tarapore 1997 (generic futures recommendation) but became too vague once reports from Ratho INR 2022 made precise recommendations about INR futures, non-deliverable forwards on exchanges, and 24×5 interbank trading through overseas branches. Specifying asset classes makes cross-report comparison more rigorous.
Example: Ratho INR 2022: "AD Cat-I banks should be permitted to carry out interbank forex transactions through overseas branches 24×5." This is specifically a currency derivative market-hours recommendation — now correctly flagged, rather than merged with interest rate derivative reforms.
DMI OTC Markets & P-Notes OTC Markets, Forward Contracts & NDF Markets Renamed + Expanded P-Notes removed (moved to FPI theme as a stand-alone sub-theme); NDF markets added. This is a substantive analytical correction: P-Notes belong under investor access (FPI), not market structure (DMI). In their place, "NDF Markets" captures the onshore-offshore NDF integration debate — central to Ratho INR 2022 and the RBI's 2025 Master Directions on FX derivatives.
Example: Tarapore 1997's OTC forward contract liberalisation and Ratho INR 2022's NDF integration are correctly grouped together; while Tarapore 2006's P-Note ban recommendation moves to the FPI theme where it analytically belongs.
DMI Settlement & Clearing Systems Settlement, Clearing & CCP Infrastructure Expanded "CCP Infrastructure" explicitly added. Central Counterparty infrastructure (CCIL upgrade, CLS inclusion for INR, tripartite repo) has emerged as the dominant theme in post-2015 reports but was being coded under a generic "clearing systems" label that didn't signal its systemic importance. FSLRC 2013 and H.R. Khan 2019 both make strong CCP-specific recommendations.
Example: FSLRC 2013: "Every clearing house should be able to settle in central bank money; mandatory for systemically important CCPs." H.R. Khan 2019's six recommendations on corporate bond CCP. The old label obscured the CCP-specific nature of these recommendations.
DMI No equivalent Corporate Bond Market & Debt Market Infrastructure NEW New Addition Entirely new sub-theme. H.R. Khan 2019's 6 recommendations on corporate bond market infrastructure (electronic dealing platforms, partial credit enhancement by banks/NBFCs, large-corporate market borrowing mandate, LAF corporate bond collateral) had no home in the old 3-sub-theme DMI structure. The corporate bond market is a critical conduit for cross-border debt flows and deserves its own sub-theme.
Example: H.R. Khan 2019: "An electronic dealing platform with CCP facility should be introduced for corporate bonds." This is debt market infrastructure reform — distinct from derivative market structure or settlement systems. Previously unclassified.
Systemic Risk Systemic Risk, Financial Stability & Macroprudential Policy — 3 old sub-themes → 4 new sub-themes
SR Banking Sector Prudential Norms Capital Adequacy, Prudential Norms & Banking Sector Safety Expanded "Capital Adequacy" moved to the front and "Safety" added. This brings the sub-theme into alignment with modern Basel III / FSLRC language. The old label focused on "prudential norms" generically; the new label foregrounds the capital adequacy dimension (Basel III countercyclical buffers, SIFI designation) as the central mechanism of banking safety — the vocabulary regulators actually use.
Example: FSLRC 2013's 17 banking prudential recommendations, including "countercyclical capital buffer as a policy tool" and "ring-fencing banks vis-à-vis non-bank entities" — these are capital adequacy interventions more precisely than simply "prudential norms."
SR Fiscal & Monetary Preconditions Fiscal & Monetary Preconditions for Capital Account Liberalisation Expanded "for Capital Account Liberalisation" added as an explicit purpose clause. The old label was descriptively correct but contextually incomplete — a reader couldn't tell why fiscal and monetary conditions appear in a systemic risk theme without the "capital account liberalisation" context. This addition makes the causal logic explicit.
Example: Tarapore 2006: "Meet FRBM fiscal consolidation targets to support fuller capital account convertibility." The purpose clause was implicit in the old schema; now it is front-and-centre in the sub-theme name itself.
SR Crisis Management & Resolution Crisis Management, Resolution & SIFI Oversight Expanded "SIFI Oversight" added. Systemically Important Financial Institution (SIFI) supervision emerged as a distinct regulatory concept in the Basel III era and is central to FSLRC 2013. The old label captured crisis response; the new label adds pre-crisis SIFI oversight — the preventive dimension — which is analytically distinct from resolution (the reactive dimension).
Example: FSLRC 2013: "FSDC approval required before the Resolution Corporation initiates any resolution action involving a SIFI." This is SIFI oversight, not just crisis management or generic resolution — a meaningful distinction now reflected in the sub-theme name.
SR No equivalent Regulatory Architecture, FSDC & Inter-Agency Coordination NEW New Addition Entirely new sub-theme. FSDC as a statutory body, SIFI conglomerate monitoring, FHC model transition, and 5-year systemic risk review mandates are governance-architecture recommendations that belong in systemic risk but fit none of the three old sub-themes (prudential norms, preconditions, or resolution). FSLRC 2013 alone generates ~8 such recommendations.
Example: FSLRC 2013: "FSDC approval before any SIFI resolution action." UK Sinha 2010: "Create a financial sector appellate tribunal." These are regulatory architecture recommendations — not banking norms, not fiscal preconditions, not resolution — but had no classification home in the old schema.

These legislative reform recommendations appeared in multiple old sub-themes (FDI "Procedural Simplification," FPI "Compliance") without any indication that they were FEMA codification recommendations.
Example: FSLRC 2013's recommendation to consolidate all capital account rules under the Indian Financial Code — previously classified under "Registration & Classification" (FPI) despite being a cross-cutting legislative reform recommendation.

UK Sinha 2010 and FSLRC 2013 both have strong recommendations on due process and appellate mechanisms — previously these sat in SR "Crisis Management" or were unclassified.
Example: UK Sinha 2010: "Create a financial sector appellate tribunal for capital flows decisions." FSLRC 2013: "FSAT as appellate forum for capital control disputes." Analytically, these are rule-of-law recommendations, not systemic risk recommendations.
No old theme — Regulatory Governance, Consultation & Transparency NEW New Theme Mandatory public consultation before capital-flow rule changes, cost-benefit analysis, duty-to-explain principle. FSLRC 2013 generates ~10 such recommendations on regulatory process quality — none of which had a classification home in the old 6-theme schema.
Example: FSLRC 2013: "Mandatory public consultation before any capital controls regulation." UK Sinha 2010: "Require transparent and approachable frameworks for all financial regulation interpretation." These are process-governance recommendations, previously unclassified.
No old theme LCI BIT 2015 was entirely unclassified (0 sub-theme cells) in old schema Bilateral Investment Treaties & International Dispute Resolution NEW New Theme The LCI BIT 2015 report had literally 0 sub-theme cells filled in the old schema — all 13 of its recommendations were unclassified. A Legal & Governance theme with a BIT/ISDS sub-theme now gives this report a home. BIT "Fair & Equitable Treatment" standards and investor-state dispute settlement directly affect how foreign capital flows are legally protected.
Example: LCI BIT 2015: "Replace the Fair & Equitable Treatment clause with a more specific protection standard." This is directly relevant to cross-border capital flows (it governs investor protections for FDI and FPI) — but the old schema had no category for it whatsoever.
Capital Account & INR Entirely New Theme — Capital Account Convertibility & Rupee Internationalisation · 4 new sub-themes
No old theme Tarapore 1997 & 2006 CAC sequencing was split across SR "Fiscal & Monetary Preconditions" and FDI "Entry Routes" Phased Capital Account Convertibility Roadmap NEW New Theme The Tarapore Phase I/II/III sequencing logic was the central architecture of both Tarapore reports, yet the old schema had no theme for "capital account convertibility" as a standalone concept — it was split across SR (preconditions) and FDI (entry routes). The new theme gives the phased CAC roadmap its own identity.
Example: Tarapore 1997's 3-phase LRS limits ($25k → $50k → $100k) and Tarapore 2006's "fuller capital account convertibility" roadmap are now classified together under a CAC Roadmap sub-theme, rather than scattered.
No old theme Ratho INR 2022 was coded under RCF/SR with significant content left unclassified Rupee Internationalisation & Trade Settlement in INR NEW New Theme Ratho INR 2022 — the most recent thematic report — generates ~16 recommendations on INR invoicing, Masala bonds, BISIP-INR, bilateral INR settlement MOUs, and SDR basket inclusion. The old schema could not accommodate these because it had no "rupee internationalisation" concept. This is now the most report-specific sub-theme in the entire schema.
Example: Ratho INR 2022: "RBI should pursue BISIP-INR with BIS and target SDR basket inclusion." These are rupee internationalisation recommendations with no ancestor in the old schema — they had to be split across SR and RCF sub-themes artificially.
No old theme — IFSC / GIFT City Development NEW New Theme IFSCA Fund Management Regulations, Family Investment Funds, GS-FPI framework, SWAGAT-FI green channel, IFSC branch guarantee exemptions — none of these had a classification home in the old schema. GIFT City is India's institutional mechanism for internationalising capital flows; it cuts across FDI, FPI, and ECB, requiring its own sub-theme rather than being scattered.
Example: SEBI's SWAGAT-FI framework (2026) — a single-window green channel for trusted foreign investors in GIFT City — involves FPI registration, FDI policy, and capital account opening simultaneously. Only a standalone IFSC sub-theme can accommodate it correctly.
No old theme — Cross-Border Payment Systems & Fintech Integration NEW New Theme UPI cross-border expansion, RTGS international use, CCIL extended hours, stablecoin risk to monetary sovereignty. These are forward-looking recommendations from Ratho INR 2022 and the FSR Dec 2025 — entirely absent from the 2013-era reports that shaped the old schema. Cross-border payment systems are now a front-line capital-flows governance issue and require dedicated classification.
Example: Ratho INR 2022: "Expanding UPI for cross-border settlements beyond current limits with FEMA documentation exemptions." FSR 2025: Stablecoin risk to monetary sovereignty. These fintech-era recommendations would have been unclassifiable in the old 6-theme schema.
