Regulator / Tax News

The reform allows FPIs to offset same-day sale proceeds against purchase obligations within the same settlement cycle, substantially reducing temporary funding and liquidity costs under the prevailing gross-settlement model. 2026-03-15 ECB / Trade Credit FDI or ECB? FEMA Treatment of Optionally Convertible Debentures (OCDs) Issued to Foreign Investors CS Akash Verma / LinkedIn Regulatory Note
Legal Analysis
 Practitioner note on the classification of Optionally Convertible Debentures (OCDs) issued by Indian companies to foreign investors under the FEMA framework: OCDs do not qualify as FDI instruments under the FEMA (Non-Debt Instruments) Rules, 2019, because the FDI-eligible instruments — equity shares, Compulsorily Convertible Preference Shares (CCPS), and Compulsorily Convertible Debentures (CCDs) — require compulsory conversion into equity without any redemption option. OCDs carry a redemption possibility, giving them a debt character at issuance, and therefore must comply with ECB regulations (eligible lenders, MAMP, end-use restrictions) if issued to foreign investors. Proper instrument structuring is essential in hybrid cross-border capital transactions to avoid FEMA non-compliance. ↗ View

 SEBI internal paper (September 2025 board meeting) proposing to allow Indian Mutual Funds to become constituents of Foreign Portfolio Investors, extending a facility already available to resident individuals. The proposal follows progressive expansion of NRI and OCI participation rights in FPI structures in GIFT-IFSC. If implemented, Indian Mutual Funds would be permitted to allocate a portion of their corpus through GIFT-IFSC-domiciled FPIs that invest in global markets, creating a regulated outward capital flow conduit. This would facilitate portfolio diversification for Indian retail investors through the mutual fund route rather than the LRS, subject to aggregate investment limits and SEBI/RBI oversight.
